# Fictional Online Degree Program Handbook

> This handbook is fictional and created only for classroom use in the workshop.
> It does not represent any real institution's official policy.

## 1. Quiz windows
Weekly quizzes open every **Friday at 6:00 PM IST** and close on **Monday at 11:59 PM IST**.
Students are expected to attempt quizzes within this window.
A missed quiz is normally treated as missed work unless an approved exception is granted.

## 2. Assignment submissions and late penalties
Assignments can be submitted up to **3 days after the deadline** with a late penalty of **10% per day**.
After 3 days, the submission link closes and no further submission is accepted through the normal workflow.

## 3. Regrading requests
If a student believes an answer was graded incorrectly, they may submit a regrading request within **5 calendar days** of score release.
The request must clearly explain which question is affected and why.

## 4. Exam rescheduling
Rescheduling for proctored exams is considered only for **medical emergencies**, **family emergencies**, or **major technical failures** documented with proof.
Students must raise the request within **72 hours** of the missed exam or as soon as reasonably possible.

## 5. Fee refunds
A learner may request:
- **100% refund** before batch activation
- **70% refund** within 7 days of activation if no live session has been attended
- **No refund** after live interactive sessions have been attended or after 7 days, whichever comes first

## 6. Technical issues
Temporary internet problems, browser crashes, or device failures should be reported with screenshots when possible.
The support team may guide the learner, but repeated technical issues do not automatically guarantee deadline extensions.

## 7. Office hours and support channels
Teaching assistant office hours are held every **Tuesday and Thursday from 7:00 PM to 8:00 PM IST**.
Routine doubts should be redirected to office hours, discussion forums, or course channels before escalation.

## 8. Academic integrity
Copying code, sharing solutions, or submitting generated work as original work without disclosure may count as an academic integrity violation.
Cases with suspected plagiarism or misconduct must always be escalated to a human reviewer.

## 9. Certificates and completion
A certificate is issued only after the learner satisfies course completion requirements, including minimum score thresholds and required assessments.

## 10. Accessibility and accommodations
Students needing accommodations should contact support with relevant documentation.
Accommodation-related cases should be escalated for human handling rather than auto-resolved.

## 11. Discussion forums
Students are encouraged to ask conceptual doubts in the forum.
Support replies should not reveal answer keys or directly solve graded assessments.

## 12. Response style guideline for support agents
Support responses should:
- acknowledge the student's issue
- state the relevant policy in plain language
- suggest the next step
- clearly mention when escalation or documentation is required
- avoid promising exceptions that are not in policy
